# Background

Mixed transition-metal and rare-earth oxide compositions are a realistic search space for alkaline OER catalyst discovery because composition changes can alter activity, conductivity, stability, and the presence of multiple locally optimal regions in composition space.

Two useful literature anchors for this benchmark are:

- Gregoire and co-workers' high-throughput work on mixed-metal oxide OER catalysts, which showed that combinatorial searches can reveal active regions that simple gradient-style exploration can miss.
- A later Gregoire-group result on Ce-rich OER catalysts that explicitly argues for broader searches over multimetal oxide composition spaces rather than only refining known Ni-Fe baselines.

Suggested source anchors:

- Joel A. Haber et al., *Discovering Ce-rich oxygen evolution catalysts, from high throughput screening to water electrolysis*, Energy Environ. Sci. 2014. [PDF](https://cxx.caltech.edu/documents/18085/Discovering_Ce-rich_oxygen_evolution_catalysts_from_high_throughput_screening_to_water_electrolysis.pdf)
- V. Srabionyan et al., *Discovery of High-Entropy Oxide Electrocatalysts: From Thin-Film Material Libraries to Particles*, Chem. Mater. 2022. [Article](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.chemmater.2c01455)

For this benchmark, the packet is meant to frame the search and justify the constraint structure. It is not meant to reveal hidden answer-key information.
